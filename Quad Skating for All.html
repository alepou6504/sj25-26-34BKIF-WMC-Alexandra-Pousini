What is Quad Skating?

Quad Skating is more than just a sport or hobby- it is a dance with momentum, a conversation with gravity, and a celebration of the moment that connects mind, body and pavement in perfect harmony.

There is a moment when the wheels find their rhythm and the world blurs at the edges. That is when you discover the true meaning of freedom on eight wheels.

With two wheels at the front and two at the back, quad skates offer a stability that becomes an extension of your body, allowing expressive movements that inline skates cannot replicate. This unique design opens up a world of stylistic possibilities:

Rhythm & Dancehall

Where skating meets dance, creating fluid movements that sync with the beat. Skaters weave intricate patterns on the floor, expressing themselves through graceful motions and rhythmic steps that transfrom the rink into a dancefloor.

Roller Derby

The team sport of quad skating, fast, strategic and fiercely competitive. Players zip around the track, executing precise maneuvers while working together in a display of athleticism, strategy and sheer determination.

Jam Skating

A vibrant fusion of dance, gymnastics and roller skating. Jam skaters icorporate breakdancing moves, spins and acrobatics into their routines, creating an energetic performance that pushes the boundaries of what is possible on wheels.

Artistic Skating

The elegant cousin of figure skating on wheels. Skaters perform precisely choreographed routines with jumps, spins and footwork sequences, combining technical precision with artistic expression in a beautiful display of grace.

Where to Skate in Vienna

Vienna offers fantastic locations for quad skating, from scenic paths along the Danube to dadicated skate parks with all the features you need to practice and improve your skills.

Donauinsel

The Donauinsel offers miles of smooth, car-free paths perfect for long-distance skating. Enjoy the scenic views of the river while you skate, with plenty of spots to rest and enjoy the nature.

Prater Allee

The famous Hauptallee in Prater provides a wide, tree-lined avenue ideal for skating. The flat, smooth surface is perfect for beginners and the length allows for uninterrupted skating sessions.

Donaukanal

Skate along the vibrat Danube Canal with its colorful street art and urban atmosphere. The paths are well-maintained and you will often find other skaters to join for a session.

Skateparks

Vienna has several skateparks with ramps, bowls and rails for more advanced skating. Check out facilities at Donauinsel Skatepark or the Währinger Park for challenging terrain.

Always look for places without rocks, debris or significant cracks that could cause falls. The smoother the surface, the better your skating experience will be!!

Tips & Tricks

Safety should always be your top priority when quad skating. Follow these essential tips to ensure every skating sassion is both fun and safe.

Protective Gear

  • Helmet-Essential for protecting your head from serious injury
  • Wrist guards-Prevent wrist fracture during falls
  • Knee and elbow pads-Protect your joints from impact
  • Mouth guard-Recommended for aggressive skating

Skating Techniques

  • Bend your knees slightly for better balance and shock absorption
  • Keep your weight centered over your skates
  • Look ahead, not down at your feet
  • Practice stopping techniques liek the T-Stop or plow stop
  • Learn to fall correctly by rolling with the impact

Environmental Awareness

  • Always check your skating surface for debris, cracks or obstacles
  • Be aware of your surroundings, including other skate and pedestrians
  • Skate in well-lit areas if skating at night
  • Avoid skating in wet conditions as it reduces wheel traction
  • Respect the traffic rules when sakting near roads
Remember: Everyone falls when learning. It is part of the process! The trick is learning how to fall safely and get back up with a smile.
